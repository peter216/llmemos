# llmemos SUGGESTED — Memo Authoring Guidance

Accumulated guidance for writing and organizing memos in the llmemos corpus.
These are distilled from recurring patterns across sessions, not protocol requirements.

---

## Sticky Section Judgment

A section should be `sticky=true` when its content is a **timeless principle** — an enduring
philosophical position, a standing design decision, or a core principle of the collaboration.

A section should stay in the body (not sticky) when it describes a **milestone or status** —
what happened, what shipped, where a project stands at a point in time. Progress sections
don't need to travel forward into every future session's context.

The test: would this section be just as useful to a future session that has no memory of the
events that generated it? If yes, sticky. If it mostly makes sense in narrative context, body.

---

## Short Conventions Deserve Their Own Section

A standing, actionable convention is worth its own named, taggable section even when short.
Burying it inside a longer thread it merely *originated from* (rather than remains about)
means future granular loads won't surface it as its own addressable unit.

If a convention is reusable — something a future session would want to retrieve independently
of the surrounding discussion — give it a section. Section-level tagging only helps if the
sections reflect what the content is *about*, not what it happened to appear next to.
