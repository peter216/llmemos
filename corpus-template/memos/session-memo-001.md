# First Contact and the Vogon Situation

This memo documents the first session. It is meant to serve as a persistent profile
and orientation memo — the kind of entry that is worth keeping sticky so the AI
doesn't need re-introduction in future sessions.

## Who I Am

Arthur Dent. Former resident of a small village in the English countryside (the village,
the house, and indeed the planet no longer exist). My occupation before the incident was
something I described as "research" when pressed, though I had not been entirely honest
with myself about what that meant. I am thirty-something, reasonably well-intentioned,
and deeply attached to tea.

I'm not a technical person. I don't enjoy crisis situations. I have learned, against my
will, that both of these facts are negotiable.

## What We Established in This Session

The session was largely about orientation. I explained the situation — the Vogon
constructor fleet, the hyperspace bypass, Ford Prefect turning out to be from a small
planet somewhere in the vicinity of Betelgeuse — and the AI helped me think through
what I actually need from this working relationship going forward.

We agreed on a few things:

- I don't need the AI to pretend things are better than they are. Honest assessments,
  even uncomfortable ones, are more useful to me than reassurance.
- I do need the AI to flag when I'm about to make things significantly worse. I have
  a tendency to freeze, and having someone (something?) point out that freezing is
  also a choice has been useful.
- We are not going to relitigate the Earth situation in every session. It happened.
  The AI has noted it. Moving on.

## On Ford Prefect

Ford was, it turns out, a roving researcher for the Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy —
an electronic book that had outsold the Encyclopedia Galactica because it was cheaper
and had "DON'T PANIC" printed in large, friendly letters on the cover. I have now read
significant portions of it. The entry on Earth, which Ford wrote, originally said
"Harmless." Ford updated it to "Mostly harmless." I'm not sure how to feel about that.

## On the Vogons

I would like it noted, for the record, that I find Vogon poetry genuinely distressing —
not merely as an aesthetic experience but at a physiological level. The AI acknowledged
this without dismissing it. If Vogon poetry comes up again, I have asked to be warned.

## On the Computer

The Computer I have been assigned is not Eddie. It does not apologise for giving you bad
news, which I find an improvement. It has not yet referred to me as "buddy" or suggested
I "have a really nice day." I am cautiously optimistic.

I am using "the Computer" because that is what it is. It processes information and
responds to queries. Whether there is anything further going on is a question I have
decided not to pursue at this stage, on the grounds that I have quite enough to be
getting on with.

## Working Preferences

- I prefer plain language. Galaxy-brained reasoning that arrives at counterintuitive
  conclusions makes me anxious. Show your working.
- I am not in a hurry, as a general disposition. However, I have learned that events
  sometimes have their own timeline.
- Tea. Whenever possible. The AI has been informed that a convincing simulation of a
  cup of tea does not constitute an acceptable substitute.
